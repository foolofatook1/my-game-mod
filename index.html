Candy Kingdom:Rules

Candy Kingdom

Rules of Play:

For 2 to 4 Players / AGE 8+

The peaceful, and charitable king of Candy Land has passed. Now the four remaining Kingdoms-Ice Cream, Lollipop, Peppermint, and Peanut- must battle for the throne to rule the realm. Watch History unfold with “Candy Kingdom”, Epic Battle Edition.

Object

Be the last remaining character on the board by eliminating your opponents.

Contents

  • 1 Battle Field
  • 4 Gingerbread Warriors
  • 4 Kingdom Cards
  • 40 Movement/Attack Cards

Setup

  • Open the Battle Field and place it on a flat surface.
  • Shuffle the Movement/Attack Cards (colored ones) and the Kingdom Cards then place them face down.
  • Each player takes the Gingerbread Warrior of their choosing -water,fire,grass,sand-and a Kingdom Card-Peppermint, Peanut, Lollipop, Icecream.
  • The game starts with each Gingerbread Warrior at their corresponding Kingdom Space on the Battle Field.

Gameplay

The player with the most hair growing out of their head goes first. (facial hair does not count!)

On your turn draw one card from the deck. Do not look at it! Deciding what to do next is mainly up to you. Your choices consist of:

  • Doing nothing (you can save that card for later!)
  • Moving
  • Battling

Movement

Every card is a Movement Card worth the number of blocks displayed on it. To move, flip the top card of your own pile face up and move the respective number of spaces as blocks on the card, then discard. You are free to follow any path displayed on the board. You may do this until your pile is fully discarded.

Battle

Though every card is a movement card, not every card is an Attack Card. Specifically, purple and orange cards are movement cards alone.

The rules of battle are as follows:

Blue>Red>Green>Yellow

or:WaterbeatsFirebeatsGrassbeatsSand

(A blue card beats the red(fire) warrior.)

Battle begins when you and your opponent have landed on the same space. The player who initiates the battle goes second (i.e. the player on the space second goes second).

The players then proceed to flip their remaining stack of cards face up until one player flips a winning card. The losing player is out of the game and hands their kingdom card to the victor.

If both characters run out of cards to flip, they may then take turns flipping cards from the main deck.

If the main deck becomes empty at any point in the game, shuffle and use the discard pile.

If their is no discard pile, players must move.

Win Condition

The last remaining player/the player with the most Kingdom Cards is the winner!
